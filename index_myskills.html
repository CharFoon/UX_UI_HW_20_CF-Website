My Story

I studied Fine Art and Graphic Design in Johannesburg (now known as Gauteng), South Africa. Since moving to Canada, I’ve worked in publishing, marketing and advertising. During these years, I worked both in teams and independently, crafting design solutions for real-world challenges facing stakeholders, their clients and their audience.

My experience in Graphic Design spans various sectors including nonprofits, technology and telecommunications, law, finance, automotive, food, gaming, retail, insurance, education, healthcare and energy in the B2B and B2C domain.

Working with companies like A&W, Arbor Memorial Inc., Adidas, American Express, Benjamin Moore Paints, Bridgestone Firestone, Brother Canada, Caesars Windsor, General Electric, Kraft Canada, Government of Canada, Puma, Toronto Hydro, Toshiba, Toyota and Wind Mobile has taught me how to work with diverse products, services and teams.

My skill set has since expanded to include UX/UI Design. What could be more exciting than flexing your design thinking muscle in a collaborative environment? From conducting user research (where empathy and having good listening skills is key) to developing user insights (illustrated through storyboarding and journey mapping). From defining and framing problems to ideating possible solutions. From wireframing to prototyping, all while conducting user testing and performing constant iterations throughout the process.

Does the process ever end? That's the beauty of UX/UI Design. It never really does. There's always room for improvement and iteration. It’s a journey. Yes, it's good, but how do we make it better?

I consider myself to be a lifelong learner, who jumps at every chance to keep learning not just in a professional, but in a personal capacity too. Empathy, respect, humility, agility and flexibility to adapt in changing environments is important to me.

Everyone has a story. Thanks for listening to mine!
